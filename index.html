        01 — Identity & Archetype
        WHO IS
        SGAUGE

        SGauge is the synthesis of contradictions held without apology. West coast melody filtered through Colorado survival. Engineering precision behind an artist's vulnerability. A father who turned the hardest year of his life into the foundation of everything he's building.

        Primary Archetype
        THE SURVIVOR SAGE

        The one who went through it and came back with receipts. Not performing pain — reporting it. The wisdom of a person who had no map and drew their own.

        Secondary Archetype
        THE BUILDER OUTLAW

        Operates outside the system while building within it. Engineer, developer, A&R, artist — simultaneously. The infrastructure guy who's also the product.

        Sonic Identity
        UPBEAT PAIN

        West coast melodic influence with a Colorado edge. Vibey enough to sing along to. Heavy enough that you feel the weight underneath. Joy and grief in the same bar.

        Influences
        THE REFERENCE POINTS

        Mike Posner, Jay Sean, A Boogie wit da Hoodie, Colbie Caillat, Grieves, Slug of Atmosphere. The ones who made something personal feel universal.

        02 — Artist Manifesto
        I come from the part of Littleton nobody admits exists. I come from a mother's addiction and a father's prison sentence and a floor that wasn't mine to sleep on. I started rapping because I had something to say and nowhere safe to say it.

        I became an engineer because I refused to wait for someone else to build my door. I became a developer because I remember what it cost to have no one in my corner. I became RAPDAD because Nelliana deserves a father who turned the worst year of his life into something that outlasts it.

        I am not performing. I am not a character. I am SGauge — the same person you'll meet in the studio, on the stage, and at the funeral. Maktub. It was written. All of it.
        03 — Career Storyline
        SAME OL'
        GAUGE

        The arc is a hood story with a survivor's ending. Not glory for glory's sake — redemption as stability. The right to stop working regular jobs and finally belong to the industry you've been building from the inside.

        I
        Act One
        THE LOST SHEEP

        East Denver born. Littleton raised. Half-homeless at 14, writing on a friend's floor. The music starts as survival — pure need to translate chaos into something that makes sense.

        II
        Act Two
        SCREAMING INTO THE VOID

        The awakening comes. He builds the infrastructure — engineering, production, A&R, media platform, artist development. He feeds everyone. And yet the universe still hasn't fully aligned.

        III
        Act Three
        GLORY RECLAIMED

        Not fame. Not a platinum plaque. Belonging. A seat at the table he helped build. The music finally reaches the people it was always meant for.

        04 — The Characters
        THE THREE
        FACES
        SGAUGE
        The Core · The Real · The Artist

        Straight Shawn with the filter off. No performance, no persona — just the duality of mixed heritage and lived experience translated into west coast-influenced Colorado sound.

        SHADY O'RAQ
        The Shadow · The Release Valve

        The part that doesn't have to be responsible. Darker, more reckless, less filtered. Not a lie — an extreme. The hood story without the hope at the end.

        IICYBABII
        The Feminine · The Contrast

        The melodic softness fully unleashed. Pure melodic songwriting unconstrained by street credibility. A creative pressure valve.

        06 — Visual Identity & Packaging
        THE LOOK OF
        UPBEAT PAIN

        Aesthetic Direction: Dark, sleek, city-built. The visual language of a person who came up in the shadows. Not loud. Not flashy. Sharp. Like a samurai who also skates. Bandana with Cartier frames and makes it feel inevitable.

        Visual References: Nighttime cityscapes. Long curly hair in a bun against a dark skyline. Scarred face lit dramatically — the scars are not hidden, they're the story.

        Photography Direction: Low-key lighting. Blue and grey tones with warm amber accents. Character over beauty. No heavy retouching. This face has been somewhere.

        07 — Marketability Profile
        WHY THIS
        LANDS
        • Self-sufficient production — No studio costs. Radio-ready output on demand. Lower barrier, faster output, full creative control.
        • Multi-role infrastructure — SGauge + RAPDAD55 + RapClass creates three entry points into the same ecosystem.
        • Built-in credibility gap — Known as engineer/developer before artist. That tension, when resolved publicly, is a story.
        • International traction early — "A.I." performing overseas. Sync licensing and international markets are natural targets.
        • Differentiated lane — Documenting survival rather than glorifying the life. A distinction for a generation increasingly tired of the performance.
        • Duality as brand pillar — Mixed race, dual city, upbeat-pain sound, engineer-artist identity. Duality is structural. It's in everything.
        • The silence as power — What SGauge doesn't say about the hardest year is more compelling than what most artists do say.
        • RAPDAD as platform multiplier — Interview access + artist development = relationships + distribution channel.
